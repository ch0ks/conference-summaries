# Establishing AI Governance Without Stifling Innovation: Lessons Learned - Billy Norwood (FFF Enterprises)

## Executive Summary
Billy Norwood, CISO of FFF Enterprises, shares the journey of implementing AI governance in a $5 billion pharmaceutical distribution company. He describes the transition from an initial consultant-led roadmap of 40 projects to a structured, tiered governance model that balances innovation with risk. Norwood emphasizes the importance of clear usage policies, specialized training, and "human-in-the-loop" requirements, especially when dealing with Protected Health Information (PHI). The talk highlights successful use cases, such as an AI agent for medical pre-authorizations that saves the company $250,000 annually.

## Key Points
- FFF Enterprises moved from a hardware/distribution focus to a tech-enabled strategy, necessitating a robust AI governance framework.
- Initial AI roadmap was overambitious; governance was needed to prioritize high-value, low-risk use cases.
- Tiered Governance Structure: A top-level committee (CISO, CIO, Legal) for policy and ethics, and an AI Center of Excellence for implementation and data science.
- High-risk cases (PHI, PII) are escalated to higher committees, while low-risk tasks can be "checked out" by managers.
- Control Plane: All AI interactions are funneled through Databricks or Microsoft Copilot to maintain a single point of visibility and control.
- AI Usage Policy: Specific lists of approved/prohibited tools and use cases, mandatory awareness training, and explicit sign-offs.
- Use Case Success: An agent that processes medical denial letters and prepares documentation for doctor review, improving patient care and reducing costs.
- "OU Spaghetti": The challenge of managing complex access controls across various organizational units and platforms.
